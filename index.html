History

Since 1977 over 250 persons have participated as directors, singers and musicians in this choir.

The Messengers for Christ Interdenominational Choir was organized in 1977 under the leadership of Ben Smith and Ed Luney. We are now moving forward as the MFC Intergenerational Choir to encompass the vast range of ages of our current membership. The choir's charter members consisted of some of the former members of the Kentucky State University Gospel Ensemble. Other directors included the late Joseph Wallace Tiggs, Sherman Lamont Bush and Kevin Brian James. Since 1989 the choir has been under the direction of Ronnie von Pierre Norfleet.

Reunion '2022'

Yes, in '2022' the choir will celebrate 45 years of music ministry.

Forty-five years is a long time for any singing aggregation. We will take this opportunity to celebrate and give thanks to God for sustaining the choir over these many years. The choir has been presenting worship and benefit concerts for many religious, educational, civic and community organizations. We are planning a wonderful weekend of activities for Labor Day Weekend, Thursday, September 1 - Monday, September 5, 2021. This weekend will include get reacquainted activiites, rehearsals, worship concert and a memorial luncheon.

Contact

It would be our pleasure for you to contact us about membership, reunion activities and our calendar of activities.

The membership of the choir covers several generations including millennials. The music repertoire of the choir is just as diverse covering genres of sacred, secular, show tunes and patriotic selections. We are accompanied by the best of musicians from the Metro-Louisville area. The choir was honored to sing as the opening choral group for the Kingdom Choir of London at the Kentucky Center for the Arts. Come and sing us! You will do us good!
